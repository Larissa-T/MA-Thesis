        <p><span style="color:#2f2a20">Undergraduate Concentrations</span></p>
<p>Brown’s Program in Medieval Studies currently offers two concentrations: <a href="http://brown.edu/academics/medieval-studies/undergraduate/concentration-medieval-cultures">Medieval Cultures</a>&nbsp;and <a href="http://brown.edu/academics/medieval-studies/undergraduate/concentration-late-antique-cultures">Late Antique Cultures</a>. A concentration in medieval cultures focuses on the sixth through the fifteenth centuries and combines interdisciplinary perspectives on this period with in-depth study of one or two related disciplines. Late antique cultures deals with the third through the ninth centuries, when ancient cultural forms were still in place but medieval cultures were beginning to take shape simultaneously.</p>
<h2>Congratulations to Vhalla Otarod, Eleanor Walsh and Gabriella Reyes finished&nbsp;with honors in&nbsp;Medieval Cultures last year.</h2>

        <h2>Concentration in Late Antique Cultures</h2>
<p>(undergraduate major)</p>
<h3>Overview</h3>
<p>The concentration in Late Antique Cultures centers on the  third through the ninth centuries inclusive and studies human  activity  in all its variety <span class="thumbnail-wrapper inline inline-right" style="width:108px;"><img src="https://www.brown.edu/academics/medieval-studies/sites/brown.edu.academics.medieval-studies/files/images/arithmetica.thumbnail.jpg" alt="Arithmetica" title="Arithmetica"  class="image image-thumbnail " width="100" height="100" /><span class="caption"><strong>Arithmetica</strong></span></span>unrestricted by the conventional demarcations of   “classical” and “medieval.” The  concentration focuses on the changes in  cultural practices, social patterns, political  and economic forms, and  artistic and literary traditions in the context of  transition and  change.</p>
<h3>Requirements</h3>
<p>Two courses, one each in Roman history (CLAS 1310 or 1320;  1320 is  recommended) and in medieval history (HIST 1030 or 1040); one 1000 or   2000 level course in an approved language; six other courses drawn from   appropriate offerings in Late Antique Cultures and approved by the   concentration adviser.</p>
<h3>Honors</h3>
<p>Honors are awarded to  students who, in addition to completing the  required courses, present a  meritorious honors thesis. The thesis  permits students to synthesize various  disciplines or interests, or to  pursue a new interest in greater depth. To be  eligible for Honors,  students must complete a minimum of six approved courses  in Late  Antique Cultures by the end of their third year with more grades of A   than B. Students should apply for admission to Honors and should meet  with  their faculty adviser(s) no later than spring of the junior year  to plan the  thesis project. Accepted candidates write the thesis in a  two-semester course  sequence under the supervision of a director and  second reader drawn from the  Medieval Studies faculty.</p>

        <h2>Concentration in Medieval Cultures</h2>
<p>(undergraduate major)</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Overview</h3>
<p>The concentration in Medieval Cultures focuses on the sixth  through the<span> fifteenth centuries inclusive and combines  interdisciplinary perspectives with </span><span>depth of study in one or two  disciplines. One traditional area of study is W</span><span>estern</span><span>&nbsp;Europe,</span><span> but  students are encouraged to work in other cultural areas such as   Byzantine, Islamic, Judaic or Slavic cultures. It is also recommended  that  concentrators take during their freshman or sophomore year the  introductory  course, </span><em>Medieval Perspectives. </em><span>Other courses  that can be applied to the concentration in Medieval Cultures are listed below.</span></p>
<h3><span class="thumbnail-wrapper inline inline-left" style="width:203px;"><img src="https://www.brown.edu/academics/medieval-studies/sites/brown.edu.academics.medieval-studies/files/images/IMG_Cloisters.preview.JPG" alt="Medieval Cultures Concentrators at the Cloisters Museum, NY" title="Medieval Cultures Concentrators at the Cloisters Museum, NY"  class="image image-preview " width="195" height="146" /><span class="caption"><strong>Medieval Cultures Concentrators at the Cloisters Museum, NY</strong></span></span>Requirements</h3>
<p>Ten courses approved by the Program in  Medieval Studies, including  two courses in medieval history and one 1000 or 2000  level course that  uses primary texts in a medieval language. Students are  invited to  discuss their plans with an appropriate faculty member of the  Program. A  concentration proposal should be prepared in consultation with the  concentration  adviser.</p>
<h3>Honors</h3>
<p>Honors are awarded to  students who, in addition to completing the  required courses, present a  meritorious honors thesis. The thesis  permits students to synthesize various  disciplines or interests, or to  pursue a new interest in greater depth. To be  eligible for Honors,  students must complete a minimum of six approved courses  in Medieval  Studies by the end of their third year with more grades of A than  B.  Students should apply for admission to Honors and should meet with their   faculty adviser(s) no later than spring of the junior year to plan the  thesis  project. Accepted candidates write the thesis in a two-semester  course sequence  under the supervision of a director and second reader  drawn from the Medieval  Studies faculty.</p>

<article id="node-278" class="main-article node">








  <div class="content">
        <p>This year's Department Undergraduate Group leader is <a href="mailto:Flannery_McIntyre@brown.edu" rel="nofollow">Flannery McIntyre</a>.</p>
<p><span>The Medieval Studies DUG’s goals for the 2016 – 2017 school year are to foster an interest in the Medieval Studies program through a variety of activities and events, to collaborate with other DUGs, such as the English and Classics DUGs, and to engage both Medieval Studies concentrators and those who are interested in program / period. &nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span>All events take place at the Annmary Brown Memorial unless noted otherwise.</span></p>
<p><span><br /></span></p>
<div></div>

</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>November TBD</strong> - Trip down the hill to the RISD Museum to view Medieval collections&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

        <h1 class="article-title  with-tabs">Sample Concentration Programs</h1>                        <div id="body-content" class="second-column column main-column">

<article id="node-238" class="main-article node">








  <div class="content">
        <p><strong>Sample Concentration Programs</strong></p>
<p>The concentration programs described below were pursued by four concentrators in Medieval Studies in past years. They provide an idea of the mix of courses selected by these students, who also wrote honors theses. These are just examples. Other students have developed different programs according to their interests.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>I.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Honors Thesis Topic: <em>Remnants of Epic Poetry in a </em>Thirteenth Century <span> </span>&nbsp;&nbsp;Zamoran Chronicle.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Courses:</p>
<ul>
<li><span>Medieval Perspectives</span></li>
<li><span><span>The World of Byzantium</span><br /></span></li>
<li><span>Survey of Late and Medieval Latin Literature</span></li>
<li><span>The Classical Tradition in the Middle Ages</span></li>
<li><span>Women in Medieval Literature</span></li>
<li><span>Gothic Art and Architecture</span></li>
<li><span>The Early Middle Ages</span></li>
<li><span>Medieval History (University of Salamanca)</span></li>
<li><span>Studies in Spanish Literature of the Middle Ages</span></li>
<li><span>Aristotle</span></li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span><br />II.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Honors Thesis Topic: </span><em>The Symbolism of the Dragon in Anglo-Saxon Literature.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Courses:</p>
<ul>
<li><span>Medieval Perspectives</span></li>
<li><span>Introduction to the Study of Medieval Texts</span></li>
<li><span>Studies in Poetry: Medieval Poets</span></li>
<li><span>Old English Language and Literature</span></li>
<li><span>Old English Literature: Beowulf</span></li>
<li><span>Introduction to Medieval Art</span></li>
<li><span>Anglo-Saxon Art</span></li>
<li><span>The High Middle Ages</span></li>
<li><span>Introduction to Jewish Mysticism</span></li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;<br /><span>III. Honors Thesis Topic: </span><em>The Classical Heroine in the Medieval World: Elements of Latin and Greek Myth in the Medieval Legend of Albina</em></p>
<p>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;Courses:</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li><span>Medieval Latin</span></li>
<li><span>History of the Crusades</span></li>
<li><span>Early Medieval Europe</span></li>
<li><span>Chaucer</span></li>
<li><span>Arts du récit, 1100-1400</span></li>
<li><span>Chaucer: Canterbury Tales</span></li>
<li><span>Old English</span></li>
<li><span>Romances of Medieval Iberia</span></li>
<li><span>Dante</span></li>
<li><span>Manuscript, Image, Middle English Text</span></li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>IV. Honors Thesis Topic: <em>Cemeteries Crowned in Ruins: A Study of Memory And the Re-use of Temples in Early Medieval Gaul</em></p>
<p>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; Courses:</p>
<ul>
<li><span>The Ideal of Self</span></li>
<li><span>Charlemagne: Conquest, Empire, and the Making of the Middle Ages</span></li>
<li><span>The Body in Medieval Art</span></li>
<li><span>Ireland 1000-1250: From Brian Boru to the English Invasion</span></li>
<li><span>Old English</span></li>
<li><span>Christianity in Late Antiquity</span></li>
<li><span>Alcuin</span></li>
<li><span>The Viking Age</span></li>
<li><span>The World of Byzantium</span></li>
<li><span>From Courtly Love to Postmodern Desire</span></li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The following themes are among those that have been persued by other concentrators:</p>
<ul>
<li><span>The Medieval Islamic World</span></li>
<li><span>Medieval Judaism</span></li>
<li><span>Spain in the Middle Ages</span></li>
<li><span>Mideival Lingustics</span></li>
<li><span>Medieval Languages and Lyric Poetry</span></li>
<li><span>Medieval Archaeology</span></li>
<li><span>Late Latin Poetry</span></li>
</ul>
